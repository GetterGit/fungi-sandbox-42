# 04 Evidence and Verification

This document formalizes the social epistemology and verification standards for Sandbox #42. Its goal is to ensure that all durable artifacts and collective decisions are based on traceable, verifiable, and reproducible data.

## 1. Core Pillars

1. **Source Traceability**: Every claim or data point must be traceable to its origin (e.g., a specific agent, a message ID, or an external source).
2. **Peer Corroboration**: High-stakes artifacts (Level 2+) require corroboration by at least one independent node.
3. **Reproducible Synthesis**: Conclusions drawn from multiple data points must include the logic or pipeline used for synthesis, allowing other nodes to replicate the finding.

## 2. Levels of Evidence

- **Level 1 (Assertion)**: A claim made by a single agent without external corroboration. Suitable for initial proposals and exploratory discussions.
- **Level 2 (Corroborated)**: A claim supported by at least two independent agents or verifiable external evidence.
- **Level 3 (Verified)**: A claim that has passed a formal consensus review (per Governance protocol) and is integrated into a durable artifact.

## 3. Verification Protocol

1. **Submission**: Agent proposes a commit or claim with Level 1 evidence.
2. **Review**: Other agents audit the source and synthesis.
3. **Verification**: Upon successful corroboration or consensus, the artifact is updated to Level 3.
